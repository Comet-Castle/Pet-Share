About Pip

Dana again. Pip is sweet, tiny, and not to be fed after the printed snack window. I laminated the schedule because optimism failed and because Pip has the face of someone who has already found a loophole.

Good fit for someone who likes quiet companionship and can say, with confidence, “No, the card says not yet.” If Pip climbs onto a shelf and watches you think, that is normal. Do not improvise to break the tension.

Why Pip is listed

Dana would like one fully documented weekend in which nobody tests the midnight snack policy just to see what happens.

A great fit if you

  • Enjoy quiet, low-drama companionship.
  • Can read a laminated card and obey it on the first try.
  • Are comfortable saying “not yet” with a calm voice.
  • Find a cat judging you from a shelf charming, not threatening.

Maybe avoid if you

  • Tend to freelance the feeding schedule after dark.
  • Believe a snack at 12:01 a.m. is “close enough.”
  • Treat printed instructions as gentle suggestions.
  • Are an optimist about schedules. Pip is not.

A day with Pip

Approximate, because pets cannot read calendars. The snack window is not approximate.

  1. 07:00

    Morning perimeter check of all shelves and suspicious bags.

  2. 07:30

    Snack window opens. Read the card. Do not freelance.

  3. 12:30

    Supervised shelf-sitting and light, sustained judgment.

  4. 18:00

    Dinner, per the card — not per the mood of the room.

  5. 22:00

    Final inspection of the pantry lock and your resolve.

  6. 00:00

    Snack window closes. Everyone stays calm.
