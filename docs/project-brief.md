# Pet Share Project Brief

Pet Share is a demo website for showing the capabilities of Sanity CMS and a Next.js frontend. This direction is intentionally high level and can change as the project develops.

## Concept

Pet Share should feel like a polished consumer marketplace for a ridiculous premise: people can lend out their pets when they need a break from them.

Users can browse fictional pet listings and imagine borrowing someone else's dog, cat, bird, or other pet for a short period.

The humor should be obvious and heavy-handed without making the interface feel sloppy. Example campaign copy:

> Has your dog thrown up in the bed recently? Lend him out for a couple days.

That kind of content could link to a fictional listing for the pet being offered.

## Demo Goals

- Demonstrate Sanity-powered structured content, editorial workflows, and reusable content models.
- Demonstrate Next.js frontend performance and modern rendering patterns.
- Demonstrate a fully responsive frontend that feels intentional on mobile, tablet, laptop, and desktop.
- Present a believable marketplace experience around an absurd idea.
- Keep the site bright, friendly, and approachable rather than cynical or harsh.

## Tone

- Satirical and silly.
- Friendly rather than mean-spirited.
- Confidently overcommitted to the joke.
- Consumer-product polished, even when the content is absurd.

## Visual Direction

- Bright, airy, and friendly.
- Modern matte color palette with cheerful, not overly glossy, colors.
- Rounded typography using Nunito Sans and Quicksand.
- Soft interface shapes.
- Plenty of white space.
- Warm, playful imagery and pet-focused content.
- Consumer-facing polish with deliberately silly copy.
- Subtle motion that makes the interface feel lively, including entry animations and gentle hover effects.
- Fully responsive layouts with no horizontal overflow, clipped content, or desktop-only interaction patterns.

The design should feel welcoming at first glance, with the satire coming through in the content and interactions.

## Content Notes

- Pet listings should be fictional.
- Copy can lean into exaggerated owner frustration, but should avoid cruelty or anything that suggests actual animal harm.
- Content should help demonstrate Sanity fields, relationships, reusable blocks, and editorial control.
